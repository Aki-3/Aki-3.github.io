      • Dumplings in China

        Chinese dumplings originated from ancient jiaozi, which is a semi-circular noodle dish with filling wrapped in a dough. As early as the Three Kingdoms period, this food was mentioned in the book "Guangya" written by Wei Zhangyi.

        Types:

        Flat at both ends and large in the middle, it's called regular dumplings.

        Jiaozi Ridge, first base, first base, like twisting Fried Dough Twists, is called Huajiao

        One big and one pointed, a specialty variety and style of Shaxian County, Fujian Province, called Fushou Dumplings

        Some areas in Shanxi are called 'flat food'.

        The fried dumplings are called "Potsticker"

      • The Origin of Dumpings in China

        The original name of Jiaoer is Jiaoer. The filling of Jiaozi is mainly divided into meat filling and plain filling. It is said that it was first invented by Zhangzhongjing, the sage of medicine in China, to treat frozen and rotten ears.

        At the end of the Eastern Han Dynasty, disasters were serious everywhere, and many people suffered from diseases. There is a famous doctor in Nanyang named Zhang Ji, whose name is Zhongjing. He has studied medical books since childhood and learned from others' strong points. He has become the founder of traditional Chinese medicine. Zhang Zhongjing not only has excellent medical skills and can cure all kinds of difficult and complicated diseases, but also has noble medical ethics. He has treated the poor and the rich seriously and saved countless lives.

        When Zhang Zhongjing was an official in Changsha, he often treated people with diseases. One year, the local plague prevailed. He built a pot at the mouth of the Yamen and gave medicine to save people, which was deeply loved by the people of Changsha. After returning home from Changsha, Zhangzhongjing walked to the Bank of his hometown of Baihe. He saw that many poor people were suffering from hunger and cold, and their ears were cold and rotten. He was very upset and determined to treat them.

        When Zhangzhongjing returned home, there were many people seeking medical treatment. He was busy, but he always remembered the poor people with broken ears. Following the way in Changsha, he asked his disciples to set up a medical shed and a cauldron on a vacant lot in Dongguan, Nanyang, and open on the winter solstice day to give medicine to the poor to treat their injuries.

        Zhang Zhongjing's medicine is called "Quhan Jiaoer Decoction". Its practice is to boil mutton and some cold dispelling herbs in a pot. After cooking, these things are fished out and chopped up. The ear shaped "Jiaoer" is wrapped with pasta. After cooking, it is distributed to patients begging for medicine. Each person has two sweet ears and a bowl of soup. After drinking Quhan decoction, people feel hot all over, their blood is unobstructed, and their ears are warm. After eating for a while, the patient's bad ears healed.

        Zhang Zhongjing gave up medicine until the 30th day of the lunar new year. On the first day of the lunar new year, people celebrate the new year and also celebrate the recovery of rotten ears. They make new year's food like jiao'er and eat it on the morning of the first day of the lunar new year. People call this kind of food "Jiaoer", "Jiaozi" or "Bianshi". It is eaten on the winter solstice and the first day of the new year to commemorate the days when Zhang Zhongjing opened the shed medicine and cured the patients.

        It has been nearly 1800 years since Zhang Zhongjing was born, but his story of "Quhan Jiaoer Decoction" has been widely spread among the people.

      • How to Make Chinese Dumplings

        01

        The dumplings are usually made with cold water and flour. Mix the flour and water together, knead them into large rough dough, cover them with dry wet gauze or towel, and leave them for about an hour.

        02

        Cut or hand picked into several small dough, and then knead them into long round strips with a diameter of about 3 cm. Cut or hand picked into small flour preparations, and roll these small flour preparations into a slightly thicker middle and thinner surrounding dumpling skin with a cylindrical rolling pin.

        03

        Meat stuffing includes three delicacies, shrimp, crab roe, sea cucumber, fish, chicken, pork, beef, mutton and so on. It is also divided into mixed vegetable stuffing and ordinary vegetable stuffing. Wrap the stuffing core with dumpling skin and pinch it into a ingot shape.

        04

        After the water is boiled, add an appropriate amount of salt. After the salt is dissolved, put the dumplings into the pot, and then cover the pot. Do not turn over, and do not order cold water until cooked. The dumplings cooked in this way will not stick to the skin or the pot, and the dumplings left in the pot will not stick.

      • Different Types

        Of Chinese Dumpings

        Dumpings in Soup

        Shandong flavor dumping with small and delicate in shape, thin in skin and full in stuffing, and delicious in taste. When serving, bring vinegar dish, sesame oil, mashed garlic, and a bowl of soup for boiling dumplings. Eat and drink while you eat, and you will have endless aftertaste.

        Rice Dumpings

        A famous kind of dumping in Nanning, Guangxi, made in the late Qing Dynasty. They were made of rice noodles as skin, wrapped with stuffing made of horseshoe and pork, and steamed. The skin is thin and white, soft and smooth, and the filling is sweet and crisp, which is tempting to taste.

        Shrimp Dumplings

        It is a Guangzhou flavor snack, uses starch dough as the skin, fresh shrimp meat, pork and other ingredients as the filling. Its shape is like a curved comb, the skin is thin and soft, the color is white and crystal, the filling of dumpling meat is faintly visible, and the filling is delicious.

      • Dumplings in Other Countries

        In many foreign cultures, dumpling is widely defined as a kind of pasta made of thin dough wrapped with meat and vegetable filling.

        Russia: Pelmeni

        It is small (2-5cm) and filled with minced beef, pork and onion, which looks like Italian dumplings.

        Thin skin wrapped fish, mushrooms and other varieties, is one of the traditional staple foods.

        Pelmeni is one of the most popular dumplings in Russia. It is usually made of flour, water and salt. The dough is used to wrap minced beef and minced pork. This kind of dumpling has different versions in various regions of Russia, but the most classic formula is to use flour, water, salt and eggs to make dough, and the filling is mainly meat.

        Historical Background

        The origin of pelmeni can be traced back to the Ural Mountains. Although the specific time of origin is not clear, it is widely consumed throughout Russia. Pelmeni is especially popular with students and office workers because of its convenience in making and eating.

        Manufacturing Method

        The dough used to make pelmeni is usually a mixture of flour, water and salt, and sometimes eggs are added to increase its toughness. The stuffing is mainly ground beef and pork, and sometimes other meat such as moose or wild boar is added. After the dough is rolled into thin slices, it is wrapped with stuffing and kneaded in half to form a small half moon shaped dumpling.

        Edible method

        Pelmeni is usually cooked in boiling water and can be served with various spices. Common condiments include sour cream, onion, vinegar and pepper. In some areas, people also dip cooked pelmeni in sour cream or soup.

        Cultural and historical significance

        Pelmeni is not only a kind of food, but also carries the culture and history of Russia. It has different names and production methods in various regions of Russia, reflecting the multicultural and geographical diversity of Russia. For example, Ural dumplings is called pelnyani, and the stuffing includes a mixture of beef, mutton and pork, sometimes with partridge eggs.

        Italy: Ravioli

        Square leather belt lace with potato, cheese, onion and tomato sauce.

        It has a historical connection with Chinese wonton and has been recorded in the 14th century Venice literature.

        Ravioli is a traditional Italian pasta, usually square or flat pillow shaped, and sometimes made into a circle, narrow roll or wide circle. Ravioli has a variety of fillings, including meat (such as ham and beef fillet) and vegetables (such as basil leaves and lemon peel), and sometimes chicken, egg yolk and other ingredients. When making, flour, eggs and water are mixed into a pastry, wrapped with stuffing and boiled. It is usually served with beef sauce, olive oil, onion, garlic and other spices.

        Ravioli has slightly different names in different regions of Italy, for example, it is called cappelletto in Emilia Romagna region. The Rene Asian style is considered the most authentic because it can best highlight the original flavor of the food. In addition, ravioli's pastry can be tinted with tomato juice or spinach juice to make it more colorful.

        Portugal: Rissóis de camarão

        Dumplings in Portugal are also called "shrimp bags". Butter dough made of flour is wrapped with shrimp, onion and parsley. It tastes rich.

        Although it is also a half moon food with pastry and stuffing, it has little to do with dumplings. Its stuffing is not pure meat and vegetables, but stir fry onion and garlic in butter, add flour and milk to get a white sauce (if the whole shrimp with shell is boiled to peel the shrimp, the shrimp soup will be retained instead of milk) and then add the semi-solid mixed with shrimp and parsley. Wrap the dumpling skin with egg liquid and bread bran and fry until golden brown. It is usually used as an appetizer or wine dish.
